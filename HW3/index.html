"We should not just passively observe artworks, but should critically analyze them, understanding how they are influenced by historical, cultural, and political contexts."

"Before the advent of photography and printing technology, viewing art was a unique, irreplaceable experience. But now, artworks can be easily replicated and disseminated, changing our relationship with art, as well as how we view the value and meaning of artworks."

"Berger analyzed the relationship between art, power, and status. Historically, art has often been used to display power and wealth. For many people, collecting art remains a way to exhibit social status and cultural refinement."

"Berger also emphasized the impact of the environment in which art is viewed on its meaning. Seeing a painting in a museum is a different experience from seeing a reproduction of it in a book or on a computer screen. The environment of viewing can alter our interpretation and response to the artwork."
